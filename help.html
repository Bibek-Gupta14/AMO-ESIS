Accessibility Help

Use the accessibility options provided by this Website to control the screen display. These options allow increasing the text spacing, changing the text size and colour scheme for clear visibility and better readability.

Text Size Icons

Following different options are provided in the form of icons which are available on the top of each page:

  • A+ or press the + (plus) key on your keyboard to increase the text size
  • A- or press the - (minus) key on your keyboard to decrease the text size
  • A to reset default text size

Changing the Colour Scheme

Changing the colour scheme refers to applying a suitable background and text colour that ensures clear readability. There are three options provided to change the colour scheme. These are:

  • A Default contrast scheme: Standard layout colors of the website.
  • A Yellow text on Black background: Applies a dark contrast theme for low light visibility.
  • A Black text on White background: High-contrast print-like visual format.

Note: Changing the colour scheme does not affect the images on the screen.

Changing the Text Spacing

Changing the spacing of text refers to increasing the white space between two letters from the default white space to improve readability for users with visual and learning impairments.

To change the text spacing:

Click on the Text Spacing button in the accessibility bar at the top to increase the white space between letters, and click again or click Reset to restore default spacing.
